What should I bring to my first appointment?
add_circle do_not_disturb_on
For your initial consultation, please bring a valid photo ID, your current insurance card, a comprehensive list of medications you are taking, and any relevant medical records or diagnostic images (X-rays, CT scans) from previous evaluations.
Do you accept my insurance provider?
add_circle do_not_disturb_on
We accept most major private insurance plans, Medicare, and several regional PPO/HMO networks. Please contact our billing office or check our 'Insurance' page for a full list of participating providers. We recommend verifying coverage with your insurer prior to your visit.
How do I manage chronic sinusitis at home?
add_circle do_not_disturb_on
Management strategies include saline nasal irrigation, using a humidifier, staying hydrated, and avoiding known allergens. However, if symptoms persist for more than 12 weeks, we recommend a specialized evaluation to discuss advanced treatment options like balloon sinuplasty.
When should I see an ENT for hearing loss?
add_circle do_not_disturb_on
You should schedule an appointment if you experience sudden hearing loss in one or both ears, persistent ringing (tinnitus), difficulty following conversations in noisy environments, or if you find yourself frequently asking others to repeat themselves.
What are the common treatments for sleep apnea?
add_circle do_not_disturb_on
Treatments range from lifestyle changes and CPAP therapy to custom oral appliances and minimally invasive surgical procedures. Our specialists will perform a diagnostic sleep study to determine the most effective approach for your specific condition.
How do I prepare for a tonsillectomy?
add_circle do_not_disturb_on
Pre-operative care includes fasting after midnight before surgery and avoiding medications like aspirin that can increase bleeding risk. We will provide a detailed instruction packet and schedule a pre-op phone call to answer any final questions.
